In the graphs above, you can see the correlation between the inverted yield curve and the onset of economic recessions in the United States. Specifically, the first graph shows the difference in yields between U.S. government bonds maturing in 10 years and U.S. government bonds maturing in 2 years. Because longer maturity bonds usually offer higher yields than shorter maturity bonds, the line is usually above the horizontal axis at zero.
